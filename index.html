Thousands of Public School Students in Washington State Head Back to Class in Schools with 'Very High' Seismic Risks

The schools in King County with "very high" seismic risks had more than 38,000 students last year, according to the most recent count in October.

Assessments conducted by state agencies to assess the risk of level of school buildings take many factors into account. As a result, the distribution of high versus low risk schools does not fall evenly along the lines of predicted impact zones. Some of the most significant risk factors are older buildings without retrofits, and location in tsunami zones. This project examines preliminary assessments of schools in an ongoing state database called ICOS.
